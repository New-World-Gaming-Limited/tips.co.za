Types of betting bonus in South Africa

Welcome deposit match

The most common offer matches your first deposit by a set percentage up to a maximum amount. A 100% match up to R1,000 means a R500 deposit becomes R1,000 in your wallet. The bonus portion cannot usually be withdrawn until wagering requirements are met. Check whether the bonus and deposit are wagered separately or together.

No-deposit free bet

A smaller number of bookmakers offer a free bet simply for creating a verified account without needing a deposit. Easybet (R50), Hollywoodbets (R25), Betfred (R50) and YesPlay (R50) all offer no-deposit bonuses. These are lower-risk than deposit matches because you are not putting your own money in before testing the product.

Deposit match plus free spins

Some bookmakers combine a sports bonus with casino free spins. TicTacBets offers 100% up to R5,000 plus 50 spins, and Gbets bundles casino promotions with its sports welcome offer. Casino and sports bonuses are often separate with different wagering rules.

Reload and weekly promotions

Beyond welcome offers, look for weekly cashback, reload bonuses and accumulator boosts. 10bet offers Monday cashback up to R1,000. Easybet provides 15% weekly cashback on net losses. These ongoing promotions are often more valuable to active bettors than a one-time welcome offer.

Promo codes

Some bonuses require a promo code at registration or on the deposit page. See the full promo code directory for current codes and verification dates.

How to read bonus terms

What a bonus is really worth

A R1,000 match bonus sounds generous. At 8x wagering, you need to place R8,000 in bets before withdrawing. At 2.0 minimum odds, roughly half your qualifying bets must win just to stay neutral. A R500 bonus at 3x wagering at 1.50 odds requires only R1,500 in bets at much easier odds to clear.

Wagering requirements

Wagering (also called turnover) is the multiplier applied to the bonus before you can withdraw. Lower is better. 1x to 3x is generous. 8x or above is high and should prompt careful reading before depositing.

Minimum odds

Most bookmakers require qualifying bets to be placed at minimum odds, typically between 1.50 and 3.0. A requirement of 3.0 odds means you must only count bets on outcomes paying R3 for every R1 staked, which is a meaningful restriction on low-scoring markets like football draws.

Expiry

Bonus expiry ranges from 24 hours (Hollywoodbets) to 30 days (Betway, 10bet). A tight expiry forces rushed betting and increases the chance of poor decision-making. Set a reminder and use your bonus intentionally rather than under pressure.

Withdrawal caps

Some bonuses cap the amount you can withdraw from winnings. Hollywoodbets caps bonus winnings at R1,200. Supabets caps at R3,000. If you plan to bet large amounts, check whether the cap restricts what you can ultimately withdraw.
