How Promo Codes Work

A promo code is a short string of letters or numbers that a bookmaker assigns to a specific promotion. You enter it during the registration process, usually in a field labelled "Promo Code", "Bonus Code", or "Referral Code". The field appears at different stages depending on the site: some show it on the first registration screen, others ask for it just before you make your first deposit.

Once you submit the code, the bookmaker's system links your account to that promotion. The bonus itself isn't always credited immediately. For deposit match offers, the bonus usually lands after your qualifying deposit clears. For free bet codes, the credit often appears within a few minutes of registration.

Each code is tied to one bookmaker and one promotion. A Betway code won't work on Hollywoodbets, and a code for a welcome bonus won't apply to an existing account. Codes also can't be combined: if a site runs two simultaneous promotions, you choose one. Read the offer page before you register so you know exactly which code applies to which bonus, and what deposit or bet you need to make to unlock it.

Types of Betting Bonuses in South Africa

South African bookmakers run several distinct bonus formats. Knowing the difference helps you pick the offer that suits your betting habits.

Free bets (no deposit required). A handful of bookmakers credit a small free bet just for completing registration, before you deposit anything. Easybet's R50 free bet is the best-known example. The amount is modest, but you risk nothing to claim it. Free bets are usually paid as a bonus bet token, not withdrawable cash. If your free bet wins, the stake is deducted and you keep the profit.

Deposit match bonuses. The most common format. You deposit a qualifying amount and the bookmaker adds a percentage of that as bonus funds. Betway's 100% match up to R1,000 means a R500 deposit gives you R500 in bonus funds to bet with, capped at R1,000 total. The bonus funds sit separately from your cash balance and can only be withdrawn after meeting wagering requirements.

Enhanced odds. A short-term price boost on a specific event or market. No promo code is needed in most cases. The enhanced price applies to your first bet or up to a capped stake. Any winnings at the enhanced price are paid as a free bet rather than cash.

Cashback offers. If your qualifying bet loses, the bookmaker refunds a percentage (or the full amount up to a cap) as a free bet or bonus credit. These reduce downside risk but usually come with wagering requirements of their own.

Wagering requirements. Almost every bonus carries a wagering requirement: a multiplier stating how many times you must bet the bonus amount before it converts to withdrawable cash. A 3x requirement on a R100 bonus means you must place R300 in qualifying bets before the balance unlocks. Minimum odds apply to qualifying bets, so very short-priced selections often don't count.

How to Claim Your Promo Code

The process is the same across most South African bookmakers. Follow these steps to make sure your code registers correctly.

  1. Pick a bookmaker from the list above. Compare the bonus value, wagering requirements, and minimum odds before deciding. The highest bonus isn't always the easiest to unlock.
  2. Click "Visit" to open the registration page. Don't sign up through a different tab or window, as the tracking link ensures the promo code is associated with your account from the start.
  3. Fill in your personal details. You'll need a valid South African ID number, your full name, date of birth, address, and contact details. All licensed bookmakers are required to collect this under FICA.
  4. Enter the promo code in the designated field. Look for a field labelled "Promo Code" or "Bonus Code" on the registration or first-deposit screen. Copy the code exactly, including capitalisation, as most codes are case-sensitive.
  5. Make a qualifying deposit if required. Deposit the minimum amount stated in the offer terms. Depositing less may mean the bonus isn't triggered, even if the code was entered correctly.
  6. Check your bonus balance. The credit should appear within minutes for free bets, and within 24 hours for deposit matches. If it doesn't arrive, contact the bookmaker's support team with your account number and the code you entered.

Promo Code Terms to Know

Before you claim any offer, it's worth reading the terms. These are the definitions you'll encounter most often.

Wagering requirement (rollover). States how many times you must bet the bonus before it's withdrawable. A 3x requirement means if you receive R300 in bonus funds, you must place R900 in qualifying bets. Higher multipliers (8x, 10x) make bonuses harder to clear.

Minimum odds. Many offers only count bets placed at odds of 1.50 (evens) or higher toward the wagering requirement. A bet on a heavy favourite at odds of 1.15 won't contribute. Check the minimum odds clause before placing your clearing bets.

Expiry period. Bonus funds and free bets expire if you don't use them within a set number of days, typically 7 to 30 days from the date of crediting. After expiry, any remaining bonus balance is forfeited automatically.

Maximum bet during wagering. Some bookmakers cap the stake you can place while clearing a bonus, often at R100 to R500 per bet. Bets above this limit may be settled at the bookmaker's discretion, and the bonus may be voided entirely.

Eligible markets. Not all bet types count toward wagering. Virtual sports, multiples with certain legs, and certain casino games are often excluded. The eligible markets are listed in each operator's full terms.

Each bookmaker's individual page on this site lists the specific terms for their current offer. Always check those before depositing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all bookmakers have promo codes?

No. Several licensed South African bookmakers don't use a promo code system at all. They may still offer a welcome bonus, but it's applied automatically to new accounts without a code. If there's no code listed for a particular bookmaker on this page, the bonus is either automatic or that site currently has no active promotion.

Can I use multiple promo codes?

No. Promo codes are tied to a single promotion per account. You can't enter two codes at once, and you can't claim a welcome bonus more than once on the same account. Some bookmakers do run ongoing reload offers or loyalty promotions after the welcome period, but those are separate from the initial sign-up code.

Do promo codes expire?

Yes, but the expiry works in two ways. The code itself may only be valid for a limited time, meaning you have to register within a certain window to use it. Once you've claimed the bonus, the funds have a separate expiry period, usually 7 to 30 days, during which you must meet the wagering requirements. We update the codes on this page regularly to remove expired offers.

What happens if I forget to enter my code?

If you completed registration without entering a code, contact the bookmaker's customer support as soon as possible, before making any deposits. Some bookmakers will manually apply the code to your account if you contact them promptly. Others won't, as their terms state the code must be entered during registration. There's no guarantee of a retroactive fix, so double-check the code field before submitting your registration form.
