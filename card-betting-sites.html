Card payments for betting: what to know

Debit cards vs credit cards

Debit cards are accepted widely and process without issue at most South African betting sites. Credit card acceptance is more variable, and some bookmakers do not accept credit cards for gambling transactions. Debit cards draw directly from your balance and are generally the easier route.

Bank blocking of gambling transactions

Some South African banks block card payments to gambling merchants by default. If your deposit is declined, check your banking app for a setting called "gambling transactions" or "merchant category blocking". FNB, Nedbank and Absa all offer these controls through their mobile apps. Disabling the block takes effect immediately in most cases.

3D Secure authentication

Most card deposits at South African betting sites require 3D Secure (3DS) authentication. You will receive an OTP to your registered mobile number to confirm the transaction. Make sure your bank has your current number on record before attempting a card deposit.

Card withdrawals

Most licensed South African bookmakers withdraw to bank accounts rather than directly to cards. If you deposit with a card, your withdrawal will typically go to the bank account associated with that card rather than back to the card itself.

Frequently asked questions

Most bookmakers do not charge deposit fees for card payments. However, your card issuer may apply a cash-advance fee if the transaction is classified as a gambling payment. Check your card's terms or contact your bank to understand how gambling transactions are categorised.
