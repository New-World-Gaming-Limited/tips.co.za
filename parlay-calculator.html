How parlays work

A parlay (also called an accumulator or multi-bet) combines two or more selections into a single wager. Every leg has to win for the bet to pay out. The appeal is simple: because the odds multiply together, the potential return is much higher than placing each bet on its own. A three-leg parlay at 2.00, 1.80, and 1.50 gives you combined odds of 5.40, turning a R100 stake into R540. But if even one leg loses, you lose the entire stake.

Most South African bookmakers call them "multi-bets" or "accumulators" rather than parlays, though the concept is the same. You'll typically need at least 2 legs to build one. The maximum number of legs varies by operator. Betway lets you go up to 20 selections, while Hollywoodbets caps it at 16. Some bookmakers also set a maximum payout on accumulators, so check the rules before building a massive multi.

Here's the catch: the more legs you add, the lower your real chance of winning. A 5-leg parlay where every selection is priced at even odds (2.00) pays 32 times your stake, but your actual probability of landing all five is only about 3.1%. The bookmaker's margin also compounds across each leg, so the house edge grows with every selection you add. Smart bettors treat parlays as an occasional bit of fun rather than a core strategy.

FAQ

What's the maximum number of legs in a parlay?

It depends on the bookmaker. Betway allows up to 20 legs in an accumulator, while Hollywoodbets caps it at 16. Other operators fall somewhere in between. You can always check the multi-bet rules in the help section of your sportsbook.

Can I cash out a parlay early?

Yes, most SA bookmakers now offer partial or full cash out on accumulators. Betway and Hollywoodbets both support this. The cash out value depends on how many legs have already won and what the current odds look like for the remaining selections. It won't always be available on every market, but for popular sports it usually is.

Do all bet types count toward parlays?

Not always. Some bookmakers exclude certain markets like player props or specials from accumulators. Standard match result and over/under markets are almost always eligible. If a market can't be combined, the site will usually warn you when you try to add it to your betslip.

Are parlays good value?

Parlays amplify both the potential return and the risk. Because the bookmaker's margin compounds across each leg, the house edge on a parlay is always higher than on a single bet. A 4-leg accumulator where each leg carries a 5% margin effectively has a 20%+ margin working against you. They're fun and exciting, but they shouldn't be your main betting strategy.
