Withdrawals and payouts at South African betting sites

A practical guide to how betting withdrawals work, including KYC requirements, processing times, common delays and how to get paid faster.

Written by Lerato Dlamini Senior Reviewer Last reviewed: April 2026 Fact-checked by Sipho Nkosi

How betting withdrawals work in South Africa

Every licensed South African bookmaker processes withdrawals through bank transfer to a verified bank account. There are no instant withdrawal options like those available in some other markets. The standard pipeline involves: requesting the withdrawal in your account cashier, a pending review period of 0 to 24 hours, and then bank processing of 1 to 3 business days.

KYC: why it matters for payouts

Know Your Customer (KYC) verification is a legal requirement for all licensed South African bookmakers under the Financial Intelligence Centre Act and gambling regulations. Before your first withdrawal, you will need to provide:

  • Identity document: A clear copy of your South African national ID (green barcoded ID or smart ID card) or a valid South African passport.
  • Proof of address: A utility bill, bank statement or government letter dated within the past 3 months, showing your full name and current address.
  • Payment method verification: Some bookmakers ask for a photo of the card or screenshot of the payment app used for your first deposit.

The fastest route to a quick first withdrawal is to submit these documents when you register rather than when you want to withdraw. Most bookmakers allow document uploads through your account profile or by emailing their compliance team.

The withdrawal reversal window

Some bookmakers allow you to cancel a withdrawal request while it is still in a pending state and return the funds to your betting balance. This window is typically between 2 and 24 hours. The reversal option is designed as a convenience feature but can be a psychological trap for players under emotional pressure after a win. If you have requested a withdrawal, consider whether cancelling it is genuinely the right decision rather than an impulse response.

What to do if a withdrawal is delayed

If your withdrawal has not arrived within the bookmaker's stated processing time, the first step is to check whether your KYC documents have been fully verified. Log in to your account and look for any outstanding document requests. If your account is fully verified, contact the bookmaker's live chat and provide your withdrawal reference number. If the bookmaker does not resolve the issue within a reasonable time, escalate to the relevant provincial gambling board.

Frequently asked questions

Most licensed South African bookmakers process withdrawals within 24 to 72 hours via bank transfer. Betway and Hollywoodbets typically pay within 24 to 48 hours. Slower operators or new accounts with incomplete verification may take up to 3 business days.

You typically need a copy of your South African national ID or passport, proof of address dated within 3 months, and sometimes proof of the deposit method used. Submit these documents as soon as you register to avoid delays when you want to withdraw.

Most bookmakers allow you to cancel a pending withdrawal within a short window, typically 2 to 24 hours. After that, the request cannot be reversed. Check your bookmaker's specific terms for the reversal window.

Common causes include: incomplete KYC documentation, a manual review triggered by the amount or betting pattern, your bank's own processing schedule, or a public holiday. Contact live chat support if your withdrawal has exceeded the stated processing time.

Most licensed bookmakers do not charge withdrawal fees. The cost is on the bookmaker's side. Your bank may apply a fee for receiving the transfer - check your bank's account terms. Betway, Hollywoodbets, Easybet and Gbets all list withdrawals as free in their cashier terms.
