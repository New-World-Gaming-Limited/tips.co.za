Understanding Value Bets

What is a Value Bet?

A value bet exists when a bookmaker's odds imply a lower chance of winning than the true probability. For example, if a bookmaker prices Arsenal at 2.10 but the actual probability of winning is 55%, that bet carries a positive expected value (EV). The EV% shown on each card is the estimated long-run profit per rand staked. A +5% EV bet should return R5 profit for every R100 wagered, over a large enough sample of bets.

How to Use These Picks

Sort by the EV badge to prioritise the highest-edge selections. Green badges (5%+) indicate the strongest value; amber badges (2-5%) are moderate edges. Click through to Betway or TicTacBets to place your bet quickly, since odds can move fast once sharp money follows. Value betting works over volume, so track your results and stick to consistent stake sizing. Never chase losses by increasing stakes on later bets in a session.
