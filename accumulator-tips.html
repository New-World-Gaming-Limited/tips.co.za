Explainer

How Accumulators Work

If you're new to acca betting, here's the mechanic in three steps.

1
Pick your selections

Choose two or more match outcomes. Each selection has its own decimal odds, for example 1.85 for a home win.

×
2
Odds multiply together

If your three selections are 1.85, 2.10, and 1.70, your combined odds are 1.85 × 2.10 × 1.70 = 6.60.

=
3
All legs must win

If any single leg loses, the whole acca loses. A R100 stake at 6.60 combined odds returns R660 if all three win.

Why we cap at 3 to 4 legs. Each leg you add multiplies the odds but also multiplies the risk. A 3-fold where each selection wins 65% of the time has a 27% combined win rate. Add a fourth at 65%, that drops to 18%. Add a fifth, and you're at 12%. The returns look attractive, but the probability falls off sharply. We keep our Safe Treble to three legs and our Value Acca to four for this reason.

FAQ

Accumulator Tips: Common Questions

An accumulator, or acca, is a single bet that combines two or more selections. All selections must win for the bet to pay out. The odds for each leg multiply together, which means even modest individual odds can produce a large combined return. A 3-fold at 2.00 per leg returns 8.00x your stake.
Three to four legs strikes the best balance between value and probability. Adding more legs increases the potential return, but the combined win probability falls sharply with each leg added. A 5-fold acca where each leg has 60% win probability has only a 7.8% combined chance of winning. We publish a Safe Treble and a Value 4-Fold for this reason.
